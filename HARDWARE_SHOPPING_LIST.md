# Hardware Shopping List - Earth Day 2026 Event

This document lists recommended hardware for the event, organized by priority and budget level.

## üéØ Essential Equipment (Must Have)

### Video
- [ ] **Webcam** (if don't already have adequate one)
  - **Recommended**: Logitech C920 HD Pro (~$70-80)
  - **Alternative**: Logitech C922 Pro Stream (~$90-100)
  - **Budget**: Generic 1080p webcam (~$30-40)
  - **Verify**: 1080p minimum, 30fps, USB connection

- [ ] **Camera Mount**
  - **Recommended**: Adjustable desk/monitor mount (~$15-25)
  - **Alternative**: Small tripod (~$20-30)
  - **Budget**: Books/boxes for positioning (FREE)

### Audio
- [ ] **USB Microphone** (if don't already have)
  - **Recommended**: Blue Yeti (~$100-130)
  - **Alternative**: Audio-Technica AT2020USB+ (~$150)
  - **Budget Option**: Fifine USB Mic (~$30-40)
  - **Minimum**: Any USB mic better than built-in laptop mic
  - **Verify**: USB connection, cardioid pattern preferred

- [ ] **Headphones** (for monitoring)
  - **Recommended**: Any closed-back headphones you already own
  - **Purchase if needed**: Sony MDR-7506 (~$100)
  - **Budget**: Any over-ear headphones (~$20-30)
  - **Avoid**: Bluetooth (latency issues)

- [ ] **Pop Filter** (Optional but recommended)
  - **Generic pop filter** (~$10-15)
  - **DIY**: Pantyhose stretched over wire hanger (FREE)

### Lighting
- [ ] **Ring Light** (if room lighting inadequate)
  - **Recommended**: 12-18" ring light with stand (~$40-60)
  - **Features**: Adjustable brightness, color temperature
  - **Alternative**: LED desk lamp (~$25-40)
  - **Budget**: Position near window for natural light (FREE)

### Connectivity
- [ ] **Ethernet Cable** (Cat5e or Cat6)
  - **Length**: Long enough to reach router from streaming location
  - **Recommended**: Cat6, 10-25 feet (~$10-15)
  - **Verify**: Physical connection to router, no WiFi

### Computer Accessories
- [ ] **Second Monitor** (Optional but very helpful)
  - **Purpose**: Monitor chat, Discord while streaming
  - **Use existing**: Any old monitor you have
  - **Purchase**: Any 1080p monitor (~$100-150)
  - **Not essential**: Can manage with one monitor + phone

## üéì Learning Your Equipment

### Recommended Practice
- **2 weeks before event**: Daily 30-minute practice sessions
- **Practice activities**:
  - Speaking into mic at different distances
  - Adjusting lighting for different times of day
  - Testing camera angles and positioning
  - Recording yourself and reviewing footage
  - Getting comfortable with equipment

### Common Issues to Test For
- [ ] Audio echo or feedback (need headphones)
- [ ] Harsh shadows on face (need better lighting)
- [ ] Camera too low (looking down) - need elevation
- [ ] Background noise (need better mic or quieter space)
- [ ] Overexposure or underexposure (adjust lighting)

## üí° Pro Tips

### Audio Quality Priority
**Audio is MORE important than video**
- People will tolerate mediocre video if audio is clear
- People will NOT tolerate poor audio even with great video
- Invest in audio first if budget is limited

### Lighting Matters
**Good lighting makes cheap cameras look professional**
- Natural window light is best (if available)
- Face the window, don't have it behind you
- Ring lights are popular for good reason (even lighting)
- Avoid harsh overhead lighting

### Test Everything
**Murphy's Law applies to streaming**
- If something can go wrong, test it first
- Have backup plans for critical equipment
- Know how to quickly solve common problems
- Practice switching to backups

## ‚ùì FAQs

**Q: Can I use my phone as the camera?**
A: Yes, with apps like EpocCam or DroidCam, but quality may be limited. Better as backup.

**Q: Do I need a green screen?**
A: No, a clean physical background is better for this event's aesthetic.

**Q: Can I use wireless headphones?**
A: Not recommended due to latency. Use wired for monitoring.

**Q: What if I can't afford any equipment?**
A: Use what you have:
- Laptop built-in camera and mic
- Quiet room with good natural light
- Ethernet connection
- This is enough to start!

**Q: Should I rent equipment?**
A: Generally not worth it for a single 4-hour event. Borrow from friends or use what you have.
